Formula to Calculate the GPA

The credit hour system is a way of calculating GPA that takes into account the amount of work that is required for each subject. For example, a subject with a credit hour of 5 is considered to be more demanding than a subject with a credit hour of 3. The GPA is then calculated by dividing the total number of points earned by the total number of credit hours.

The credit hour system can be confusing for students because it is not always clear how many credit hours each subject is worth. The NEB has published a list of credit hours for each subject, but this list can be difficult to find and understand.
