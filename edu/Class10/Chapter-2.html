Chapter 2: Ethical and Social Issues in ICT

Class 10 Computer Science - Important Concepts for SEE Examination

Cyber Ethics

Cyber ethics refers to the moral principles and rules that guide our behavior when using computers and the internet. It's about doing the right thing online, like respecting others' privacy and not using technology to harm others.

Do's and Don'ts of Cyber Ethics

Do's:

  • Respect others' privacy and personal information.
  • Use strong passwords and protect your accounts.
  • Report any illegal or harmful activities online.
  • Be polite and respectful in online communication.
  • Use the internet responsibly for learning and communication.

Don'ts:

  • Don't share personal information or passwords with others.
  • Don't engage in cyberbullying or spread false information.
  • Don't download or share copyrighted material without permission.
  • Don't use someone else's identity or pretend to be someone else online.
  • Don't access or modify someone else's data without permission.

Computer Ethics

Computer ethics are the moral principles that govern the use of computers and technology. It involves ensuring that technology is used fairly and responsibly.

Commandments of Computer Ethics

  1. You shall not use a computer to harm other people.
  2. You shall not interfere with other people's computer work.
  3. You shall not snoop around in other people's computer files.
  4. You shall not use a computer to steal.
  5. You shall not use a computer to bear false witness.
  6. You shall not use or copy software for which you have not paid.
  7. You shall not use other people's computer resources without authorization.
  8. You shall not appropriate other people's intellectual output.
  9. You shall think about the social consequences of the program you write.
  10. You shall use a computer in ways that show consideration and respect.

Digital Citizenship

Digital citizenship is the responsible use of technology and the internet. It means being aware of your online behavior, understanding your rights and responsibilities, and acting in a way that promotes a positive online community.

Elements of Digital Citizenship

Digital Access

Ensuring everyone has equal access to technology.

Digital Communication

Using digital tools to communicate responsibly and respectfully.

Digital Literacy

Understanding how to use technology safely and effectively.

  • Digital Etiquette: Following the rules of polite and respectful online behavior.
  • Digital Law: Understanding and following the laws related to digital activities.
  • Digital Rights and Responsibilities: Knowing your rights online and respecting others' rights.
  • Digital Security: Protecting your information and devices from threats.
  • Digital Health and Wellness: Maintaining a healthy balance between online and offline life.

Digital Footprint

A digital footprint is the trail of data you leave behind when you use the internet. It includes everything you post online, websites you visit, and information you share.

Think Before You Post

Be mindful of what you share online.

Use Privacy Settings

Control who can see your information on social media.

Delete Unwanted Accounts

Close accounts you no longer use.

  • Regularly check your online presence: Search for yourself online to see what others can find about you.
  • Be cautious with apps and websites: Only use trusted apps and websites that protect your information.

Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is the use of technology to harass, threaten, or embarrass someone. It can happen through social media, text messages, emails, or other online platforms.

Common Places Where Cyberbullying Occurs

  • Social Media: Platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter.
  • Messaging Apps: Text messages, WhatsApp, or Snapchat.
  • Online Forums and Chat Rooms: Places where people discuss topics and can be targeted.
  • Gaming Communities: Online games where players can communicate.

Examples of Cyberbullying

Mean Messages

Sending mean or threatening messages

Impersonation

Pretending to be someone else online

Embarrassing Content

Sharing embarrassing photos or videos

Cyber Law

Cyber law refers to the laws and regulations that govern the use of the internet and technology. It covers issues like online privacy, hacking, and intellectual property rights.

Areas Covered by Cyber Law

  • Digital Signature Law: Laws that recognize electronic signatures as legally binding.
  • Copyright Law: Laws that protect the rights of creators over their digital content.
  • Trademark Law: Laws that protect brand names, logos, and symbols online.
  • Telecommunication Law: Regulations that govern internet service providers and communication technologies.
  • Data Protection and Privacy Law: Laws that protect individuals' data and privacy online.

Cyber Law of Nepal

In Nepal, cyber law is governed by the Electronic Transaction Act 2063 B.S. This act provides the legal framework for electronic transactions, digital signatures, and protecting online rights.

Objectives of the Electronic Transaction Act 2063 B.S.

  • To promote E-commerce: Encourage the use of electronic transactions in business.
  • To secure Digital Signatures: Provide legal recognition for electronic signatures.
  • To prevent Cybercrime: Establish laws to prevent and punish online crimes.
  • To protect Online Privacy: Safeguard personal data and privacy in digital spaces.

Social Media

Social Media refers to online platforms where people can create, share, and engage with content, such as Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and LinkedIn.

Opportunities in Social Media:

  • Communication: Connect with friends, family, and new people worldwide.
  • Business Promotion: Market products and services to a large audience.
  • Learning and Sharing Knowledge: Access information and educational resources.
  • Building a Personal Brand: Showcase your skills, achievements, and interests.
  • Networking: Create professional connections and find job opportunities.

Threats in Social Media:

  • Privacy Risks: Sharing too much personal information can lead to identity theft.
  • Cyberbullying: Being targeted by harmful or abusive behavior online.
  • Addiction: Spending too much time on social media can affect mental health and productivity.
  • Misinformation: False or misleading information can spread quickly on social media.
  • Scams and Fraud: Criminals may use social media to trick people into giving money or personal details.

What is cyberbullying? Mention any four measures to prevent it.

Answer: Cyberbullying is the use of technology to harass, threaten, or embarrass someone. It can happen through social media, text messages, emails, or other online platforms.

Four measures to prevent cyberbullying:

  1. Never share personal information or passwords online.
  2. Think carefully before posting anything that could hurt someone.
  3. Report any cyberbullying incidents to parents, teachers, or authorities.
  4. Use privacy settings to control who can see your information.
